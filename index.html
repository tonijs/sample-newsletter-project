Blanket Knit-A-Long : Fly Away

Well, a blanket KAL in the summer seems to be becoming a tradition around here! And of course modular is key due to potential summer heat and to accommodate summer vacation travel, cottage time, or lugging to and from the hammock in the yard!

This year's obsession of a blanket could hardly be easier! You have probably seen us pondering the options in the shop! Yep that is right... By the wonderful Tin Can Knits ladies comes the Fly Away Blankie. This pattern is written up as a baby blanket, but up sizing is simple thing to do. And we can help with math.

This is a garter stitch pattern, with a colour stripe (or more if you choose) in each square. The pattern was inspired by the Flying Geese Quilt Motif and the blanket is knit one square at a time, joined together, and then the border is picked up to knit out. One of the amazing things about this blanket is that you can configure the squares into a variety of different graphic effects!

Be sure to check out the project page on Ravelry to start getting inspired!
